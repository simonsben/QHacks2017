Flag is a simple web application designed to be used anywhere in the world, and by anyone. This app aims to change the way we interact and receive service in the real world. In rapidly evolving times, it is up to us to transform and develope technology that makes our daily activities easier, and more convenient. Flag aims to accomplish this by allowing users to join any "room" that they find useful. In each room is a set of flags, allowing the user to "flag" themselves, showing that they require instant service. The best part? These flags can be set to represent anything you want! Be it academic assisstance in your school, service at a restaurant, or help in a hospital, Flag will find out where you are, allow you to enter a relevant room, and notify any staff who use this app that you require service.

    

Flag allows businesses to offer unique and efficient service, giving companies a leading edge in fast, quality service. Restaurants that use devices such as tablets will no longer need to purchase expensive hardware and configure that hardware with the restaurant. Flag allows users to sign into restaurant rooms with their own user ID's, and simply "flag" themselves for instant service as specified by you. This means that your business will be able to digitize by simply installing the app! Flag is cost efficient, easy to use, and enhances service dramatically for your needs.



Flag was created by a team of four, and was designed to completely revolutionize the world around us, further integrating phones and communications technologies with the real world. The product was made by Cooper Harasyn, Ben Simons, Daniel Armitage, and Zohaib Qureshi during a Queens University Hackathon. These four engineering and computing students strive in their work, and aim to provide high quality updates and supports to ensure a user friendly, easy to use instant service application.
